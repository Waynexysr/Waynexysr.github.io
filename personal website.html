About

Zheng (Wayne) Luo

罗铮 · MS Biostatistics, NYU · Incoming PhD, CUHK

I am a master's student in Biostatistics at New York University, and an incoming PhD candidate in Medical Sciences at the Chinese University of Hong Kong, where I will be supervised by Prof. Andrea Luk.

My research focuses on health disparities and the social determinants of young-onset metabolic conditions in East Asian adults. I draw on methods from epidemiology, machine learning, and causal inference — including latent profile analysis, MAIHDA, and Blinder-Oaxaca decomposition — to understand how upstream social factors shape metabolic risk across populations.

Previously, I worked as a research assistant at NYU on SDOH and Type 2 diabetes self-management, at INSEAD on CEO cognition and corporate strategy, and at Shenzhen Bay Laboratory on computational biophysics.

Research Interests

Social determinants of health

How upstream socio-environmental factors drive metabolic risk and health inequity in East Asian populations.

Young-onset metabolic disease

Epidemiology, risk profiling, and prediction of early-onset diabetes and related conditions.

Health disparities

Multilevel and intersectional analysis of inequity using MAIHDA and Blinder-Oaxaca decomposition.

Clinical predictive modeling

Machine learning models for disease risk prediction, with emphasis on interpretability and deployment in resource-limited settings.
